The Brown Bear

The browvn bear (Ursus arctos) is native to parts of northern Eurasia and North America. Its
conservation status is currently "Least Concerm." There are many subspecies within the brown
bear species. including the Atlas bear and the Himalayan brown bear.

    While the brown bear's range has shrunk, and it has faced local extinctions across its wide range, it remains listed as a least concern species by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) with a total estimated population in 2017 of 110,000.

    The following countries have the largest populations of brown bears:

    1. Russia
    2. United States
    3. Canada
    4. Indonesia
